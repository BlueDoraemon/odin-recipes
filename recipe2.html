Fish Fingers and Custard

Are you a fan of the Eleventh Doctor and his quirky culinary habits? If you've ever been curious about Fish Fingers and Custard, the iconic dish from the episode "The Eleventh Hour," then this recipe is for you! This sweet and savory treat is a perfect representation of the Eleventh Doctor's unique personality and taste preferences, and is sure to delight any Doctor Who fan. So put on your bowtie and grab a spoon, because it's time to try this out-of-this-world recipe.

Ingredients

  • 6-8 fish fingers
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 package instant custard mix
  • 2 cups milk

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a shallow dish, mix together the flour, salt, black pepper, and paprika.
  3. In another shallow dish, whisk together the milk and egg.
  4. Dip each fish finger into the flour mixture, then into the milk and egg mixture, and then back into the flour mixture to coat it evenly.
  5. Heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add the coated fish fingers to the skillet and fry them for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they're golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove the fish fingers from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil.
  8. In a separate bowl, prepare the instant custard mix according to the package instructions using 2 cups of milk.
  9. Serve the fish fingers hot with a side of custard for dipping.
  10. Enjoy your Fish Fingers and Custard, and let the flavors transport you through time and space!
